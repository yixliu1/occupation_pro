<h1>Occupation Name: Sales and Marketing Manager</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 131112</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: AIM</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Sales and Marketing Manager plans, organises, directs, controls and coordinates the sales and marketing activities within an organisation.</p>
<p>Sales and Marketing Manager may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>directing the development and implementation of sales strategies and setting sales targets in order to maximise an organisation's sales and customer loyalty</li>
<li>directing the development and implementation of strategies to promote an organisation's goods and services to as many people as possible</li>
<li>directing the development and implementation of strategies to generate increased consumption of an organisation's goods and services through the creation and reinforcement of 'brand image' or 'brand loyalty'</li>
<li>directing the development and implementation of strategies to build and maintain an organisation's image and reputation with its customers, investors and the wider public</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
