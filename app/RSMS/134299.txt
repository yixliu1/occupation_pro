<h1>Occupation Name: Health and Welfare Services Managers</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 134299</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Health and Welfare Services Managers plan, organise, direct, control and coordinate the professional and administrative aspects of health and welfare programs and services.</p>
<p>This occupation group covers Health and Welfare Services Managers not elsewhere classified. Occupations in this group include:</p>
<p>They may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>Director of Pharmacy</li>
<li>Director of Physiotherapy Services</li>
<li>Director of Speech Pathology</li>
<li>Manager of Allied Health Services Medical Corps Officer (Army)</li>
<li>providing overall direction and management for the service, facility, organisation or centre</li>
<li>developing, implementing and monitoring procedures, policies and standards for medical, nursing, allied health and administrative staff</li>
<li>coordinating and administering health and welfare programs and clinical services</li>
<li>monitoring and evaluating resources devoted to health, welfare, recreation, housing, employment, training and other community facilities and centres</li>
<li>controlling administrative operations such as budget planning, report preparation, expenditure on supplies, equipment and services</li>
<li>liaising with other health and welfare providers, boards and funding bodies to discuss areas of health and welfare service cooperation and coordination</li>
<li>advising government bodies about measures to improve health and welfare services and facilities</li>
<li>representing the organisation in negotiations, and at conventions, seminars, public hearings and forums</li>
<li>controlling selection, training and supervision of staff</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Health and Welfare Services Manager is classified as Group A occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group A occupations is to hold qualification(s) assessed at Australian Bachelor degree or higher degree with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment in the last five years.</p>
