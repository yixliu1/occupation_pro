<h1>Occupation Name: Archivist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 224211</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Archivist analyses and documents records, and plans and organises systems and procedures for the safekeeping of records and historically valuable documents.</p>
<p>Archivists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>determine how long records need to be kept for accountability and historical purposes by following specified record-keeping requirements</li>
<li>ensure records necessary for the ongoing operations of an organisation are identified and preserved</li>
<li>survey records held by client agencies and individuals, and arrange for their transfer to archival custody</li>
<li>collect records in accordance with an acquisitions policy</li>
<li>establish and manage digitisation initiatives to make records more accessible online</li>
<li>plan for and carry out digital preservation activities on 'born digital' records (materials that originate in digital form)</li>
<li>design systems (including hardware, software, procedures and manuals) that enable organisations to create and keep records of their business activities</li>
<li>establish and manage administrative systems to document and control records and archives</li>
<li>compile guides, inventories and indices to assist reference and research</li>
<li>provide access to records through reading rooms and online for research, administrative, legal and other purposes</li>
<li>provide advice and information on issues such as copyright and privacy</li>
<li>assist users by explaining how to retrieve information from the records</li>
<li>research publications or prepare exhibitions using archival records</li>
<li>promote collections and engage with users through educational programmes, exhibitions, publications and the web</li>
<li>ensure that the environmental conditions required for the storage and conservation of records are maintained according to scientific methods</li>
<li>advise on record management issues.</li>
