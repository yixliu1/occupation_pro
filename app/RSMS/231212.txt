<h1>Occupation Name: Ship's Engineer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 231212</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: AMSA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Ship's Engineer controls and manages the operation and maintenance of a ship's plant and equipment. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Ship's engineers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>ensure that the main engines and associated machinery, electrical, refrigeration, air conditioning and cargo-handling equipment on ships are operating efficiently</li>
<li>monitor automatic data recording equipment, or record readings manually on ships without such equipment</li>
<li>analyse the readings for trends and make adjustments to machinery operation as required</li>
<li>carry out regular maintenance of the main engine and repair defective auxiliary equipment</li>
<li>stand watch as officer in charge of the engineering watch at sea and in port</li>
<li>ensure that vessel operations do not create pollution</li>
<li>maintain engine room stores and record usage of parts and equipment</li>
<li>supervise staff and carry out major maintenance work while the ship is in port</li>
<li>be involved with the operation of small craft such as patrol boats, tugs, river ferries and port authority vessels.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation is on the Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme Occupation List (RSMS ROL) , you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclass:</p>
<li>Regional Sponsor Migration Scheme (subclass 187)</li>
