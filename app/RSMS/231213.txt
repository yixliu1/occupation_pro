<h1>Occupation Name: Ship’s Master</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 231213</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: AMSA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Ship’s Master controls and manages the operation and maintenance of a ship's plant and equipment. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Ships' masters may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>control and direct the ship during berthing and mooring</li>
<li>plan navigation according to relevant rules and regulations, allowing for possible emergencies during the voyage</li>
<li>ensure safety regulations are adhered to</li>
<li>oversee the receipt and transmission of information by radio, flags, morse lighting and pyrotechnic lighting</li>
<li>ensure the ship runs safely, efficiently and economically</li>
<li>monitor and maintain procedures for fire hazard, abandon ship and damage control situations</li>
<li>observe and record the ship's course, any route alterations, the weather conditions and the ship's position in the daily logbook</li>
<li>coordinate cargo loading and discharge operations</li>
<li>oversee maintenance activities</li>
<li>take responsibility for budgeting and expenditure.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Regional Occupation List (ROL). Regional Occupation List consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in regional states and territories of Australia.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on ROL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) Medium-term stream</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
