<h1>Occupation Name: Marine Surveyor</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 231215</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: AMSA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Marine Surveyor surveys machines and hulls of ships to ensure they are constructed, equipped and maintained according to safety standards, rules and regulations laid down by marine authorities. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Marine surveyors may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>examine and approve design plans of hulls and equipment, such as main propulsion engines, auxiliary boilers and turbines, electrical power generating plant, refrigeration and air conditioning plant and pumping systems</li>
<li>inspect standards of construction and witness tests of materials</li>
<li>inspect hulls, machinery and equipment during ship construction to ensure standards and legislative requirements are met</li>
<li>conduct surveys throughout the ship's life to ensure standards are maintained</li>
<li>perform inspections required by domestic statutes and international conventions</li>
<li>witness tests and operation of emergency and safety machinery and equipment</li>
<li>measure ships for tonnage and survey them for load line assignment</li>
<li>attend court as an expert witness and assist with coronial enquiries</li>
<li>investigate marine accidents.</li>
