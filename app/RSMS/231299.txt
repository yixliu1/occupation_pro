<h1>Occupation Name: Marine Transport Professionals nec</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 231299</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>This occupation group covers Marine Transport Professionals not elsewhere classified. Occupations in this group include:Boating Safety Officer (Aus), Marine Safety Officer, Vessel Traffic Officer (Aus)</p>
<p>Marine Transport Professionals may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>directing fishing operations by using knowledge about the species sought, fishing areas, seasons and the capabilities of the vessel and crew</li>
<li>directing crew in catching fish, molluscs and crustacea at varying depths using nets, lines, poles, pots and traps</li>
<li>planning, controlling and coordinating the operational and maintenance requirements of a ship's propulsion and domestic plant and equipment</li>
<li>operating plant and equipment and performing routine maintenance on ship's systems including mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, steam generating, and fire prevention and control systems</li>
<li>controlling and directing shipping operations to ensure the safe and efficient loading and transport of cargo and passengers</li>
<li>ensuring compliance with regulations pertaining to safety at sea and protection of the marine environment</li>
<li>directing the activities of the deck crew for navigational support tasks, berthing and unberthing, maintenance, cleaning and painting of superstructures, and repair and replacement of defective deck gear and equipment</li>
<li>navigating a ship by supervising the ship's course and speed according to predetermined passage plans and safety procedures</li>
<li>examining and approving design plans of hulls and equipment such as main propulsion engines, auxiliary boilers and turbines, electrical power generating plant, refrigeration and airconditioning plant and pumping systems</li>
<li>conducting periodic surveys throughout a ship's life to ensure standards are maintained</li>
