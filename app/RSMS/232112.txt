<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Landscape Architect plans and designs land areas for projects such as open space networks, parks, schools, institutions, roads, external areas for all building types, land subdivisions, and commercial, industrial and residential sites.</p>
<p>Landscape architects may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>study and discuss designs, costs and construction of projects with clients</li>
<li>talk to architects, engineers and other professionals, and gather information on factors such as historical and natural conservation requirements, soil structure, drainage, rock features, existing and proposed buildings and sun and shade movements</li>
<li>draw up site plans outlining site development, discuss plans with clients and seek approval</li>
<li>prepare specifications, arrange cost estimates, list building materials required and detail working drawings of the site, showing features such as location of buildings, roads and walkways; land contours and drainage systems; soil conservation measures; and the vegetation to be planted, retained or removed</li>
<li>investigate the heritage of sites and make plans for the management of open space areas</li>
<li>use computer and video simulation packages to develop broadscale landscape plans</li>
<li>supervise site work</li>
<li>advise on landscape problems concerned with environmental planning.</li>

<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Landscape Architect is classified as Group A occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group A occupations is to hold qualification(s) assessed at Australian Bachelor degree or higher degree with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment in the last five years.</p>
<p>According to Vetassess, a highly relevant field of study is Landscape Architecture, which is the study of planning, designing and installing exterior environments with concern for functionality, practicality and human needs and requirements. It includes the design of parks and gardens integrated with the built environment (ASCED). Degrees in Architectural Studies without a major in Landscape Architecture will not be assessed positively, unless the applicant has official registration as a Landscape Architect. This occupation requires an applicant to undertake highly relevant employment as a Landscape Architec</p>
