<h1>Occupation Name: Cartographer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 232213</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Cartographer applies scientific, mathematical and cartographic design principles to prepare and revise maps, charts and other forms of cartographic output.</p>
<p>They may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>determining the position of points of interest on the earth's surface including marine floors, and preparing the final product data in digital form</li>
<li>supervising the preparation of plans, maps, charts and drawings to give pictorial representations and managing spatial information systems</li>
<li>undertaking research and development of surveying and photogrammetric measurement systems, cadastral systems and land information systems</li>
<li>planning and designing land subdivision projects and negotiating details with local governments and other authorities</li>
<li>advising Architects, Engineering Professionals, environmental and other scientists or other relevant professionals on the technical requirements of surveying, mapping and spatial information systems</li>
<li>compiling and evaluating data, interpreting codes of practice, and writing reports concerning survey measurement, land use and tenure</li>
<li>preparing site plans and survey reports required for conveyancing and land ownership matters</li>
<li>evaluating, compiling and maintaining spatial information using a range of digital and graphical source materials, including aerial photographs, satellite imagery, survey documents, existing maps and records, historical data, reports and statistics</li>
<li>analysing and interpreting data to design maps, graphs, plans, drawings and three-dimensional models using geographic information and related systems</li>
<li>developing and trialling new applications for use in geographic information systems</li>
<li>supervising and coordinating the work of Surveying or Spatial Science Technicians in the production and reproduction of geographic products</li>
