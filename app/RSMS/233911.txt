<h1>Occupation Name: Aeronautical Engineer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 233911</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: Engineers Australia</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Aeronautical Engineer performs and supervises engineering work concerned with the design, development, manufacture, maintenance and modification of aircraft for flight.</p>
<p>Aeronautical Engineer may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>undertaking systematic manufacturing, involving the assembly and modification of components;</li>
<li>applying the principles of science and technology to create aircraft, components and support equipment;</li>
<li>supervising the assembly of airframes and the installation of engines, instruments and other equipment;</li>
<li>maintaining aircraft for full operation including making regular inspections, maintenance and servicing;</li>
<li>measuring and improving the performance of aircraft, components and systems;</li>
<li>developing repair procedures as well as working out and managing schedules for repair and maintenance;</li>
<li>investigating aircraft accidents;</li>
<li>collating information, interpreting data and publishing the results of specific projects in technical report form;</li>
<li>working with teams, suppliers, clients and managers to agree budgets, timescales and specifications;</li>
<li>project managing, including scheduling resources and staff and managing budgets;</li>
