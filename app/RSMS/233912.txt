<h1>Occupation Name: Agricultural Engineer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 233912</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: Engineers Australia</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Agricultural Engineer performs and supervises engineering work related to the use and development of agricultural land, buildings, machines and equipment. Registration or licensing may be required.</p>
<p> </p>
<p>Agricultural engineers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>plan, supervise and manage the building of irrigation, drainage, flood and water control systems</li>
<li>design, develop and manage the manufacture of agricultural machinery, equipment and instrumentation, such as sensing, measuring and recording devices</li>
<li>plan and supervise the construction of farm and other related buildings, such as controlled environments (intensively housed livestock, greenhouses, nurseries and aquaculture, for example) and storage facilities (including grain silos and dryers)</li>
<li>supervise ground preparation, seeding and harvesting, spray technology, post-harvesting (processing and packaging) and transport equipment</li>
<li>supervise the cleaning, grading, milling, mixing, food processing, packaging and distribution of produce</li>
<li>perform environmental impact assessments</li>
<li>analyse, advise and plan for effective soil conservation and the control of water logging and soil salinity</li>
<li>conduct research and study the results of work on farms, forests and research stations</li>
<li>prepare and present reports.</li>
