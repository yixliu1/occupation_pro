<h1>Occupation Name: Biomedical Engineer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 233913</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: Engineers Australia</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Biomedical Engineer applies knowledge and methodology of physics, engineering, mathematics, computing, physical chemistry and materials science to problems in biology and the treatment and prevention of human disease. Registration or licensing may be required.</p>
<p>Biomedical Engineers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>design new medical monitoring, diagnostic and therapeutic equipment</li>
<li>set up and maintain medical equipment</li>
<li>specify equipment for particular purposes</li>
<li>test the safety, efficiency and effectiveness of equipment</li>
<li>plan data processing services and the development of associated computing programmes</li>
<li>analyse new medical procedures to forecast likely outcomes</li>
<li>participate in medical or scientific procedures where biomedical skills are needed</li>
<li>design and deliver technology to assist people with disability</li>
<li>analyse and design prosthetic and orthotic devices</li>
<li>measure and monitor physiological systems</li>
<li>diagnose and interpret bioelectric data using signal processing techniques</li>
<li>provide computer analysis of patient-related data.</li>
