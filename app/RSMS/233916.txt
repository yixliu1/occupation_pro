<h1>Occupation Name: Naval Architect</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 233916</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: Engineers Australia</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Naval Architect designs and oversees the construction and repair of marine craft and floating structures. Registration or licensing may be required.</p>
<p>Naval architects may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>prepare preliminary designs by consulting with clients such as ship owners, ship builders, shipping organisations and maritime research institutes</li>
<li>estimate the initial vessel construction costs and lifetime running costs of a vessel</li>
<li>determine the most suitable type and size for a vessel and ensure proposed designs meet performance and cost requirements</li>
<li>determine the proportions and shape of the hull (body) of the vessel</li>
<li>design accommodation and cargo areas</li>
<li>make calculations relating to the structural and mechanical aspects of design, construction and repair</li>
<li>make calculations relating to the stability of the vessel and prepare the stability book to go on board the vessel to be used by the master and officers</li>
<li>supervise other people who prepare detailed designs, specifications and building contracts</li>
<li>coordinate the work of other engineers</li>
<li>obtain plan approval and supervise construction work</li>
<li>plan, supervise and evaluate dockside and sea trials of the vessel</li>
<li>survey vessels</li>
<li>organise repairs and modifications to vessels</li>
<li>research efficient ways for vessels to move through water</li>
<li>provide risk assessment and claims management for insurance.</li>
