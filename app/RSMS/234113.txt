<h1>Occupation Name: Forester</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 234113</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Forester studies, develops and manages forest areas to maintain commercial and recreational uses, conserve flora and fauna, and protect against fire, pests and diseases.</p>
<p>Foresters may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>manage forest resources by protecting and regenerating forests and promoting effective growth</li>
<li>establish plantations of native and introduced timber species</li>
<li>plan and implement fire prevention, detection and suppression systems</li>
<li>observe the effects of environmental factors on the growth and health of plants</li>
<li>measure and record tree volume and growth, as well as changes in the forest's timber and other resources</li>
<li>estimate the sustainable supply of timber and associated forest products</li>
<li>plan and implement the growing and harvesting of native and introduced plantation forests</li>
<li>protect wildlife habitats and forest streams</li>
<li>develop recreational facilities and control their use</li>
<li>undertake landscape management, which may involve manual and satellite mapping, aerial photo interpretation and field measurements</li>
<li>advise governments, industry and landholders on forestry, and plan and conduct educational programmes on the care and value of trees and forests</li>
<li>conduct research on various aspects of forestry</li>
<li>supervise and train technical, clerical and field staff and coordinate their work.</li>
