<h1>Occupation Name: Geologist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 234411</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Geologist studies the composition, structure and other physical attributes of the earth to increase scientific knowledge and to develop practical applications in fields such as mineral exploitation, civil engineering, environmental protection and rehabilitation of land after mining.</p>
<p>Â </p>
<p>Geologists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>explore specific areas of the earth to determine its structure and the types of rocks or minerals that exist</li>
<li>study rock cores, cuttings and samples</li>
<li>study geostatistics and sampling theory</li>
<li>study fossilised life forms and date rock strata</li>
<li>study the nature and effects of natural events such as erosion, sedimentation, glaciation, earthquakes and volcanic hazards</li>
<li>locate and manage groundwater resources, investigate groundwater contamination and land salinity</li>
<li>undertake geochemical sampling of stream sediment and soils</li>
<li>undertake ground magnetic and gravity surveys</li>
<li>examine geological specimens in laboratories using optical and electron microscopes, X-ray diffraction and chemical and mechanical techniques</li>
<li>assist in determining the economic viability of extracting earth resources</li>
<li>advise on the geological suitability of sites for structures such as tunnels, roads, coastal installations, bridges and water supply schemes</li>
<li>contribute information about land use, planning and rehabilitation, and the effects of pollution on seabeds to environmental assessments</li>
<li>use computers to integrate and interpret data sets of geological information</li>
<li>prepare geological models to describe processes and predict future situations</li>
<li>prepare geological reports and maps.</li>

<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
