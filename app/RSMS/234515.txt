<h1>Occupation Name: Botanist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 234515</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Botanist studies the anatomy, physiology, biochemistry and ecology of plants.</p>
<p>Botanists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>manage scientific collections of plant specimens</li>
<li>document and analyse plant biodiversity and the evolutionary origins of plants</li>
<li>investigate the effects of environmental factors such as rainfall, temperature, sunlight, soil, topography (surface features) and disease on plant growth</li>
<li>grow plants under controlled conditions to assess the significance of environmental and genetic variables</li>
<li>study the genetics of plants using biochemical and molecular techniques in a laboratory in order to determine the patterns of plant evolution</li>
<li>study the nature of plant chromosomes, cells and tissues</li>
<li>prepare scientific reports and papers</li>
<li>work with other scientists to develop medicines and other products from plants</li>
<li>search for and classify new species of plants</li>
<li>identify plant specimens and prepare handbooks for plant identification</li>
<li>use computers for information storage and analysis of data.</li>
