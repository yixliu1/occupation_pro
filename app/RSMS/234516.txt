<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Marine Biologist studies the anatomy, physiology, functions, characteristics, behaviour and environments of all forms of life living in the sea and connected water bodies.</p>
<p>Marine biologists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>estimate the number of marine organisms and analyse their population features</li>
<li>observe communities of marine organisms and determine the factors influencing their structure</li>
<li>assess and advise on the causes, effects, prevention and control of introduced species</li>
<li>use numerical and statistical skills to design laboratory and field experiments</li>
<li>design and carry out environmental impact assessments to determine whether change is caused by natural or human factors</li>
<li>participate in studies aimed at predicting the effects of proposed developments</li>
<li>develop long-term programmes for monitoring environmental pollution</li>
<li>provide guidance to help manage fisheries</li>
<li>provide information and recommendations for the development of marine conservation and harvesting policies and programmes, including aquaculture</li>
<li>write scientific reports on research and investigations and prepare more general information for scientific, managerial, political and general audiences</li>
<li>provide advice to managers, politicians, primary producers, healthcare workers and the general public</li>
<li>provide research training for students and staff seeking entry to this field.</li>
