<h1>Occupation Name: Primary School Teacher</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 241213</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: AITSL</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Primary School Teacher Teaches and coordinates a range of subjects within a prescribed curriculum to primary school students.</p>
<p>Primary school teachers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>prepare daily and longer-term lesson plans in accordance with curriculum guidelines</li>
<li>teach a full range of learning areas, including English (reading, writing, speaking and listening), mathematics, science, technology, society and environment, creative arts, personal development, health and physical education</li>
<li>develop children's interests, abilities and coordination using creative activities such as art, music and sport</li>
<li>use computers to assist with lesson preparation, teaching and reporting</li>
<li>develop and maintain good working habits and discipline in classrooms</li>
<li>carry out administrative duties</li>
<li>supervise students during classes and at other times in the school day, including in the playground during breaks</li>
<li>attend staff meetings and other training and development sessions</li>
<li>take part in joint decision-making about educational issues</li>
<li>assess and evaluate students' progress in written and oral work</li>
<li>discuss students' progress and concerns with parents and administrators</li>
<li>assist with and organise sporting activities, school concerts and excursions</li>
<li>prepare for and attend parent-teacher interviews and other functions.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
