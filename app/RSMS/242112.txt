<h1>Occupation Name: University Tutor</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 242112</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>University Tutor conducts tutorials in one or more subjects within a prescribed course of study at a university. Registration or licensing may be required.Â </p>
<p>University Tutor may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>preparing and delivering lectures, and conducting tutorials, seminars and laboratory sessions</li>
<li>preparing and marking essays, assignments, and examinations</li>
<li>advising students on academic and related matters</li>
<li>attending departmental and faculty meetings, conferences and seminars</li>
<li>supervising work programs of postgraduate and honours students and tutorial staff</li>
<li>participating in setting course and degree requirements, curriculum revision and academic planning</li>
<li>serving on council, senate, faculty and other committees and professorial boards</li>
<li>conducting research and undertaking consultancies in a particular field of knowledge</li>
<li>stimulating and guiding class discussions</li>
<li>compiling bibliographies of specialised materials for reading assignments</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation is on the Regional Sponsored Migration Scheme Occupation List (RSMS ROL), you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclass:</p>
<li>Regional Sponsor Migration Scheme (subclass 187)</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>University Tutor is classified as Group A occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group A occupations is to hold qualification(s) assessed at Australian Bachelor degree or higher degree with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment in the last five years.</p>
