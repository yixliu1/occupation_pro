<h1>Occupation Name: Dietitian</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 251111</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: DAA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Dietitian applies the science of human nutrition to help people understand the relationship between food and health and make appropriate dietary choices to attain and maintain health, and to prevent and treat illness and disease.</p>
<p>Dietitians may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>collect, organise and assess data relating to the health and nutritional status of individuals, groups and communities</li>
<li>interpret and communicate scientific information, advice, education and professional opinion to individuals, groups and communities</li>
<li>manage nutrition care for individuals by planning appropriate diets and menus</li>
<li>educate people about their individual nutritional needs and methods of accessing and preparing their food</li>
<li>manage food service systems to provide safe and nutritious food by designing nutritionally appropriate menus and implementing nutrition policies</li>
<li>plan, evaluate and implement nutrition programmes with individuals, groups or communities as part of a team (this may be in a community health, public health or food industry setting)</li>
<li>conduct food-related and nutrition-related research and evaluate practice.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
