<h1>Occupation Name: Nutritionist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 251112</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Nutritionist integrates, disseminates and applies knowledge drawn from the relevant sciences to enhance positive effects of food on the health and well-being of human populations.</p>
<p>They may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>planning diets and menus, and instructing people on the requirements and importance of diet and on the planning and preparation of food</li>
<li>collecting, organising and assessing data relating to health and nutritional status of individuals, groups and communities</li>
<li>interpreting and communicating scientific information and providing advice, education and professional opinion to individuals, groups and communities</li>
<li>monitoring food intake and quality to provide nutritional care</li>
<li>calculating nutritional values of food served</li>
<li>planning, conducting and evaluating nutrition intervention programs and compiling educational material</li>
<li>providing nutrition assessments, nutrition management, and nutrition education, research and training</li>
<li>consulting with other Health Professionals and related workers to manage the dietary and nutritional needs of patients</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Nutritionist is classified as Group A occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group A occupations is to hold qualification(s) assessed at Australian Bachelor degree or higher degree with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment in the last five years.</p>
