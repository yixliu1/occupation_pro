<h1>Occupation Name: Orthoptist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 251412</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Orthoptist diagnoses and manages eye movement disorders and associated sensory deficiencies. Registration or licensing</p>
<p>Orthoptists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>measure and assess vision and prescribe glasses when appropriate</li>
<li>investigate, monitor and assist with treating eye disorders such as glaucoma, cataracts, diabetic eye disease and age-related macular degeneration</li>
<li>measure and select intraocular (within the eye) lenses for cataract surgery</li>
<li>detect and manage eye movement disorders, such as strabismus (turned eye) and amblyopia (lazy eye), and their sensory consequences</li>
<li>conduct specialised diagnostic and imaging techniques, such as ultrasonography and topography, to determine the effects and progression of eye disease</li>
<li>assess and provide management strategies for people with neurological disorders</li>
<li>devise strategies for rehabilitating impaired vision and design therapy programmes to maximise an individual's remaining vision</li>
<li>provide reports on the existence of eye defects to practitioners, therapists and relevant authorities, such as transport authorities</li>
<li>provide educational information to patients, families and members of the community about eye conditions and their effects</li>
<li>assist the community by providing vision screening tests and promoting preventative eye health care.</li>
