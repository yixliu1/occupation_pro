<h1>Occupation Name: Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioner</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 252214</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: Chinese Medicine Board </h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioner treats imbalances of energy flows through the body by assessing the whole person and using techniques and methods such as acupuncture, Chinese herbal medicine, massage, diet, exercise and breathing therapy. Registration or licensing may be required.</p>
<p>Chinese Medicine Practitioners may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>diagnose health problems through discussions with the patient; checking the patient's pulse and tongue; and observing abnormalities in sleep, appetite, perspiration and body temperature</li>
<li>formulate traditional Chinese medicine treatment plans based on the patient's diagnosis</li>
<li>prescribe medicinal substances derived from roots, flowers, seeds and leaves in the form of teas, capsules, tinctures or powders</li>
<li>advise on dietary and lifestyle choices</li>
<li>apply other therapies such as acupuncture, cupping (applying a heated cup to the skin to create suction), tui na, and exercise and breathing therapy.</li>
