<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Audiologist provides diagnostic assessment and rehabilitative services related to human hearing defects. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Audiologists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>establish a client's problems with hearing and listening by conducting interviews and studying background information (such as medical history)</li>
<li>assess the extent of hearing loss and location of the problem using a wide range of techniques, including audiometric tests such as air and bone conduction tests</li>
<li>report results of assessment and testing in writing and make referrals to medical practitioners</li>
<li>provide rehabilitation programmes, such as counselling and guidance, for the hearing-impaired and their families</li>
<li>assess hearing levels of workers, such as pilots or members of the armed forces, when required by employers</li>
<li>prescribe, fit and evaluate hearing aids and other devices, such as cochlear implants</li>
<li>provide training in communication strategies such as lip reading and in the use of auditory and other devices</li>
<li>conduct follow-up consultations after hearing aids have been fitted to ensure clients receive the clearest amplification</li>
<li>assist with the development and management of noise control and hearing conservation strategies in industry</li>
<li>act as consultants on industrial compensation claims for work-related hearing loss</li>
<li>undertake scientific research related to sound and hearing</li>
<li>teach the science of audiology and its practice</li>
<li>direct projects and act as consultants to other professional groups</li>
<li>supervise student audiologists.</li>
