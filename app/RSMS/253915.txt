<h1>Occupation Name: Pathologist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 253915</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: MedBA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Pathologist identifies the cause and processes of disease and illness by examining changes in body tissue and in blood and other body fluids, and conducts tests on samples of tissues, blood and body secretions. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Pathologists may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>study the nature, cause, development and clinical management of diseases in people, and the structural and functional changes caused by them</li>
<li>prepare, or supervise the preparation of, tissue sections from surgical and diagnostic cases and autopsies</li>
<li>examine bodily fluids and tissues for the presence and measurement of chemical substances, microbiological organisms (such as HIV and Hepatitis C), and chemical and other biological responses to disease processes</li>
<li>examine tissues using scientific techniques and equipment to determine the nature, cause and progression of disease</li>
<li>perform autopsies to determine causes of death, the nature and extent of disease and injury, and the effect of treatment</li>
<li>supervise and coordinate the work of technical officers and technicians</li>
<li>write reports of findings for use by other medical practitioners and coroners</li>
<li>direct the activities of pathology departments in hospitals, private clinics or laboratories, or other locations.</li>
