<h1>Occupation Name: Nurse Researcher</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 254212</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: ANMAC</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Nurse Researcher assists with the design, implementation and assessment of education programs; delivery of education and staff development programs; the management of educational resources.</p>
<p>Nurse Researcher may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>researching, planning, developing and implementing nursing curricula</li>
<li>facilitating practical experience for general and specialist nurses</li>
<li>evaluating ongoing and changing educational needs and monitoring course outcomes</li>
<li>participating in developing and implementing policies affecting nursing, nurse education and health</li>
<li>undertaking and promoting nursing and interdisciplinary research projects, and disseminating research information</li>
<li>promoting utilisation of current research findings into clinical nursing practice and patient management</li>
<li>promoting the implementation of research and research findings into organisation-wide functions such as safety, quality and risk management</li>
<li>providing support and education for other nurses undertaking research</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
