<h1>Occupation Name: Systems Administrator</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 262113</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: ACS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Systems Administrator plans, develops, installs, troubleshoots, maintains and supports an operating system and associated server hardware, software and databases ensuring optimum system integrity, security, backup and performance.</p>
<p>Systems Administrator may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>designing and maintaining database architecture, data structures, tables, dictionaries and naming conventions to ensure the accuracy and completeness of all data master files</li>
<li>performing the operational establishment and preventive maintenance of backups, recovery procedures, and enforcing security and integrity controls</li>
<li>implementing and administering database documentation, guidelines, policies and procedures</li>
<li>testing database systems and upgrades, such as debugging, tracking, reproduction, logging and resolving all identified problems, according to approved quality testing scripts, procedures and processes</li>
<li>accepting responsibility for the processes, procedures and operational management associated with system security and disaster recovery planning</li>
<li>liaising with security vendors, suppliers, service providers and external resources; analysing, recommending, installing and maintaining software security applications; and monitoring contractual obligations, performance delivery and service level agreements</li>
<li>troubleshooting and providing service support in diagnosing, resolving and repairing server-related hardware and software malfunctions, encompassing workstations and communication infrastructure</li>
<li>preparing and maintaining documentation, policies and instructions, and recording and detailing operational procedures and system logs</li>
<li>ensuring that the design of computer sites allows all components to fit together and work properly, and monitoring and adjusting the performance of networks</li>
<li>continually surveying the current computer site to determine future network needs and making recommendations for enhancements in the implementation of future servers and networks</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
