<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Magistrate hears criminal matters to determine whether defendants will be committed for trial and adjudicates minor criminal offences without a jury.</p>
<p>Magistrate may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>researching statutes and previous court decisions relevant to cases</li>
<li>conducting trials and hearings</li>
<li>calling and questioning witnesses</li>
<li>hearing and evaluating arguments and evidence in civil and criminal summary matters</li>
<li>deciding penalties and sentences within statutory limits, such as fines, bonds and detention, awarding damages in civil matters, and issuing court orders</li>
<li>exercising arbitral powers if resolution is not achieved or seems improbable through conciliation</li>
<li>preparing settlement memoranda and obtaining signatures of parties</li>
<li>advising government of legal, constitutional and parliamentary matters and drafting bills and attending committee meetings during consideration of bills</li>
<li>advising clients and agents on legal and technical matters</li>
