<h1>Occupation Name: Drug and Alcohol Counsellor</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 272112</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Drug and Alcohol Counsellor provides support and treatment for people with drug and alcohol dependency problems, develops strategies which assist them to set goals and affect and maintain change, and provides community education. May work in a call centre.</p>
<p>Alcohol and drug counsellor may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>provide support to explore alternatives with clients who experience difficulties with alcohol and/or drugs</li>
<li>provide alcohol and/or other drug withdrawal services</li>
<li>work with clients who are under the influence or alcohol and/or drugs</li>
<li>provide emergency response intervention</li>
<li>provide needle exchange services</li>
<li>assist with the implementation of health promotion and community intervention and education programs on drug and alcohol awareness</li>
<li>identify and help children and young people at risk of harm</li>
<li>provide information and support on health, wellbeing and parenting</li>
<li>provide emergency response to individuals at risk of self-harm or suicide</li>
<li>act as an advocate on behalf of clients</li>
<li>assist with the evaluation and preparation of reports requesting funding for programs and new projects</li>
<li>arrange clients to be referred to other appropriate specialist professionals or support workers.\par</li>

<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Drug and Alcohol Counsellor is classified as Group A occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group A occupations is to hold qualification(s) assessed at Australian Bachelor degree or higher degree with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment in the last five years.</p>
<p>According to Vetassess, A Drug and Alcohol Counsellor provides support and treatment for people with drug and alcohol dependency problems, develops strategies which assist them to set goals and affect and maintain change, and provides community education (ANZSCO). A highly relevant field of study would be Counselling, which is the study of the practice and skills required to provide guidance on personal, social and psychological problems (ASCED). Qualifications in Psychology without studies and supervised practical training in counselling will not usually be assessed positively.</p>
