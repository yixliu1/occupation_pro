<h1>Occupation Name: Interpreter</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 272412</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: NAATI</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Interpreter transfers a spoken or signed language into another spoken or signed language, usually within a limited time frame in the presence of the participants requiring the translation.</p>
<p>Interpreters may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>interpret verbal or signed communications between two parties</li>
<li>provide simultaneous or consecutive interpretations of conversations or speeches</li>
<li>express the meaning and feeling of what is said or signed in another language in the appropriate tone and style within a range of settings</li>
<li>orally translate written texts.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>The assessment authority for this occupation is National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) and NAATI skills assessment will assess if your educational qualifications and work experience are at a professional level and closely related to your nominated occupation for migration purposes.</p>
<p>You are required to receive a positive assessment for your qualifications and work experience from National Accreditation Authority for Translators and Interpreters (NAATI) if you are planning to migrate to Australia through </p>
<p>skill migration program</p>
