<h1>Occupation Name: Blacksmith</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 322111</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Blacksmith shapes bars, rods and blocks of metal by heating and hammering to produce or repair metal articles.</p>
<p>A blacksmith may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>selecting metal stock for job requirements</li>
<li>heating metal in forges and furnaces and hammering, punching and cutting metal using hand tools and machine presses</li>
<li>tempering and hardening finished articles by quenching in oil or water baths or by cooling gradually in air</li>
<li>preparing electrolytic and silver solutions for electroforming, and applying solution to the objects to be coated</li>
<li>setting and adjusting controls to regulate electric current and depositing of coating on objects</li>
<li>preparing horses' hooves for shoeing, nailing horseshoes to hooves, and trimming hooves</li>
<li>cutting, trimming, shaping and smoothing stock to form mould patterns</li>
<li>filling boxes with sand and setting patterns in place, and pouring molten metal into moulds</li>
<li>applying refractory paint and positioning cores in moulds</li>
<li>finishing metal and articles by polishing and buffing and applying shellac, lacquer, paint and other finishes</li>
