<h1>Occupation Name: Farrier</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 322113</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Farrier inspects, trims and shapes horses' hooves, and forms, fits and nails horseshoes.</p>
<p>Farrier may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>inspect horses' legs and hooves while they are stationary as well as in motion to check for irregularities, interference, peculiarities in gait (how the horse walks) or abnormalities in size and shape of hooves</li>
<li>talk to the horse owners or trainers to decide on types of shoes and nails required</li>
<li>remove worn or faulty shoes and note wear patterns, foreign bodies, bruising, infections or deformities</li>
<li>examine, clean, trim and shape hooves using knives, hoof cutters and rasps</li>
<li>measure hooves, take a template of each hoof on a piece of cardboard and estimate the length of metal required for the shoes</li>
<li>select and cut metal according to type, size and weight of shoes and type of horse</li>
<li>hold shoes against hooves to determine the amount of shaping required</li>
<li>heat shoes in a forge, shape them on anvils and hammer them to size</li>
<li>place and nail shoes to hooves and check that clips and clenched nails are smooth and lined up with the walls of each hoof</li>
<li>remove steel shoes from horses before they race, replace with light aluminium shoes or 'tips' and replace the steel shoes after the race</li>
<li>work in the areas of corrective or surgical shoeing.</li>
