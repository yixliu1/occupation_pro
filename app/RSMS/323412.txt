<h1>Occupation Name: Toolmaker</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 323412</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Toolmaker makes and repairs tools, dies, jigs, fixtures and other precision parts and equipment to fine tolerances for machine tools and other production machinery.</p>
<p>Toolmaker may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>studying drawings and specifications to determine dimensions and tolerances of articles to be manufactured and models to be constructed</li>
<li>measuring and marking out metal stock and castings using various gauges</li>
<li>shaping metal and wood stock using machine tools</li>
<li>checking accuracy of manufactured articles and finished patterns to fine tolerances, using precision measuring instruments</li>
<li>testing and modifying manufactured articles</li>
<li>applying protective finishes to patterns and painting pattern sections to indicate method of assembly</li>
<li>assembling pattern sections and shaping work pieces to specified finish</li>
<li>pouring and spreading materials into moulds and over models of patterns, and building laminations of fibreglass cloth and plastic resin to fabricate patterns</li>
<li>repairing broken and damaged patterns and correcting patterns to compensate for defects in casting</li>
<li>constructing templates for layout and inspection</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
