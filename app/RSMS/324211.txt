<h1>Occupation Name: Vehicle Body Builder</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 324211</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Vehicle Body Builder manufactures and repairs prototype production units and purpose-built vehicle bodies such as buses, trucks, fire engines and caravans.</p>
<p>Vehicle body builders may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>manufacture framework sections in metal, wood, fibreglass and other materials by working from engineering drawings, and by using shaping machinery and all types of welding and cutting equipment</li>
<li>form complete frameworks by welding or bolting sections to the vehicle's frame</li>
<li>make the panels used to cover the framework by cutting, shaping and attaching panels of sheetmetal, aluminium, stainless steel or reinforced plastic</li>
<li>fit axles, suspensions and brakes</li>
<li>design and fit mechanised equipment, such as winches and hoists, to various types of vehicle chassis</li>
<li>install door handles, locks and hinges</li>
<li>design and carry out chassis alterations in consultation with engineers, or alter assembly line vehicles to special requirements.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
