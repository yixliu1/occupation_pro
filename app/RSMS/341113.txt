<h1>Occupation Name: Lift Mechanic</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 341113</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Lift Mechanic designs, installs, maintains, services and repairs electric and hydraulic passenger and freight lifts, escalators, moving walkways and other lift equipment. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Lift mechanics may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>study drawings and lay out the position of steelwork, guide rails, motors, pumps and cylinders</li>
<li>install lift guide rails and check that they are correctly aligned</li>
<li>install cables, counterweights, pumps, motor foundations, escalator drives, lift cars, doors, entrance frames and safety and control devices</li>
<li>connect electrical wiring to control panels and electric motors</li>
<li>test and adjust assemblies, including cables, wiring and electric controls, and adjust safety devices such as brakes and speed governors</li>
<li>carry out regular maintenance programmes on lifts and escalators</li>
<li>use laptop computers in the field for tuning and diagnostic work in fault finding</li>
<li>find the causes of faults in motors, brakes, switches and electrical and electronic control systems</li>
<li>repair hydraulic or mechanical brakes by adjusting or replacing valves, ratchets, seals and brake linings.</li>
