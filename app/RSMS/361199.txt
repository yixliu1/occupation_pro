<h1>Occupation Name: Animal Attendants and Trainers (nec)</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 361199</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>This occupation group covers Animal Attendants and Trainers not elsewhere classified.Occupations in this group include: </p>
<p>Dog Trainer or Handler, 361112 Horse Trainer, 361113 Pet Groomer, 361114 Zookeeper and 361115 Kennel Hand.</p>
<p>Animal Attendants and Trainers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>teaching animals to obey verbal and non-verbal commands and addressing behavioural problems</li>
<li>training animals to accept riders and pull vehicles</li>
<li>training animals to perform in competitions</li>
<li>bathing, cutting, combing, blow-drying and styling pets' coats, clipping their nails and cleaning their ears</li>
<li>inspecting, preparing, cleaning, disinfecting and maintaining comfortable animal cages and enclosures</li>
<li>transporting food, filling water troughs and feeding animals according to their individual needs</li>
<li>maintaining animal health records, treating minor injuries and reporting serious conditions to Veterinarians</li>
<li>exercising and playing with animals, answering visitor questions, and transferring animals between enclosures by leading or carrying them</li>
<li>dusting and spraying insecticides on animals and immersing them in insecticide baths, to control insect pests</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
