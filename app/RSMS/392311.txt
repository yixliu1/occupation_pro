<h1>Occupation Name: Printing Machinist</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 392311</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Printing Machinist produces books, magazines, newspapers, brochures, posters, leaflets, packaging materials and stationery using printing presses.</p>
<p>Printing Machinist may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>setting, adjusting and monitoring substrate-feed mechanisms, delivery mechanisms, inking systems and other printing machine functions</li>
<li>mixing ink and solvents to standard, and regulating paper and ink supply during print runs</li>
<li>monitoring, evaluating and determining press operations manually and by computer to check print quality standards against proofs and detect malfunctions</li>
<li>producing a variety of printed products using relief, lithographic, flexographic and gravure printing presses, and in-line finishing systems</li>
<li>preparing plates, blankets and impression cylinders on small offset lithographic printing presses</li>
<li>loading paper into feeding mechanisms</li>
<li>monitoring machine operations and quality of printing</li>
<li>undertaking maintenance, adjustment, repair and cleaning of machines</li>
<li>producing and managing digital print images, and transferring and outputting images</li>
<li>may set up and operate paper and bookbinding guillotines</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
