<h1>Occupation Name: Clothing Patternmaker</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 393212</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Clothing Patternmaker draws sets of master patterns following sketches, sample articles and design specifications, and cuts out patterns for garments.</p>
<p>Clothing patternmakers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>interpret a design sketch</li>
<li>choose the correct 'block' (a pattern shape that forms the basic characteristics of a garment)</li>
<li>make adjustments to the pattern to arrive at a sample pattern, which is then used to decide whether to produce the garment in bulk</li>
<li>check the accuracy of the sample pattern</li>
<li>work with the sample machinist at various stages throughout the process, such as cutting out the sample garment and determining the amount of fabric used</li>
<li>advise on the correct machining technique to use</li>
<li>check that the sample garment maintains the look of the original design</li>
<li>check the fit of the sample on a mannequin, or on a model employed by the firm</li>
<li>modify sample garments to meet cost specifications and manufacturing limitations</li>
<li>once it is approved for production, reproduce the sample pattern on a pattern board to make a master pattern</li>
<li>create the required range of sizes by 'grading' the pattern, usually using a computer-aided design (CAD) system.</li>
