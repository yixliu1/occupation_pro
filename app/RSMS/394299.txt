<h1>Occupation Name: Wood machinists and other Wood Trades Workers (nec)</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 394299</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>This occupation group covers Wood Machinists and Wood Trades Workers not elsewhere classified. Occupations in this group include </p>
<p>Cane Furniture Maker ,Cooper, Wood Model Maker.</p>
<p>Wood machinists and other Wood Trades Workers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>studying drawings, work orders and sample parts to determine specifications</li>
<li>determining tooling and machine requirements and sequence of operations</li>
<li>setting up woodworking machines and wood stock for correct cutting, planning, turning, shaping and sanding</li>
<li>operating machines to cut, plane, turn, shape and sand work pieces</li>
<li>removing old finishes by stripping with steel wool and glasspaper, and by applying solvents and paint strippers, and removing softened finishes by scraping</li>
<li>applying varnish, shellac, lacquer, stains and paint to surfaces and polishing and waxing finished surfaces</li>
<li>fitting and fastening frame pieces</li>
<li>mounting backing materials and subjects for framing</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
