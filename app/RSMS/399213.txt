<h1>Occupation Name: Power Generation Plant Operator</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 399213</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: TRA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Power Generation Plant Operator operates boilers, turbogenerators and associated plant to generate electrical power. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Power generation plant operators may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>control the flow of fuel to boilers and make sure that the machinery is working properly</li>
<li>control the flow of water in hydroelectric power stations and ensure that the machinery is working correctly</li>
<li>operate control instruments to switch off lines or equipment and to connect alternative circuits</li>
<li>check instruments and switchboards to make sure that all systems are working efficiently to detect line disturbances and to check the flow of power</li>
<li>find faults and perform basic repairs in mechanical, electrical and process control equipment and coordinate specialist repairs</li>
<li>shut down or start up boilers or turbines to decrease or increase power output</li>
<li>operate power switches for safety of crews and to avoid interruptions during repairs</li>
<li>undertake safe work procedures to isolate plant machinery and equipment for maintenance and repair</li>
<li>compile records and reports on equipment performance, instrument readings and switching operations</li>
<li>clean, lubricate and repair equipment</li>
<li>control processes and equipment associated with inputs for power generation such as fuel, chemicals and water</li>
<li>control processes and equipment associated with management of by-products to minimise their environmental impact</li>
<li>respond to emergencies such as fire and environmental hazards.</li>
