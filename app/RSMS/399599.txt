<h1>Occupation Name: Performing Arts Technicians (nec)</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 399599</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>This occupation group covers Performing Arts Technicians not elsewhere classified. Occupations in this group include: Microphone</p>
<p> Boom Operator, Performing Arts Road Manager, Continuity Person, Special Effects Person, Theatrical Dresser.</p>
<p>Performing Arts Technicians may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>operating microwave equipment to transmit video information to transmitter sites and receiving video signals from remote locations</li>
<li>maintaining and repairing radio and television transmitters and associated equipment</li>
<li>selecting and attaching equipment to cameras, positioning cameras, and following the action of scenes being photographed while adjusting controls</li>
<li>positioning equipment, such as spotlights, floodlights and cables, and operating lights during filming, broadcasting and stage performances</li>
<li>applying and retouching make up during shooting and performance, including special effects make up such as scars and wounds</li>
<li>designing and making musical instruments and instrument parts using specially selected materials and techniques similar to those used in cabinetmaking, metal pipe making, silversmithing and wood carving, and tuning and repairing musical instruments</li>
<li>setting up and adjusting equipment such as microphones, and operating sound mixing consoles and associated equipment to regulate volume and sound quality</li>
<li>selecting and setting up television recording, editing and mixing equipment, and adjusting and monitoring their operation</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Performing Arts Technician is classified as Group D occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group D occupations are the following:</p>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate IV with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate IV without a highly relevant field of study and at least two years of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate III with a highly relevant field of study and at least three years of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<p>According to Vetassess, if you have pre-qualification employment and if all of your employment is before your qualifications assessed at the AQF Certificate III or Certificate IV level), three years of highly relevant employment in addition to at least one year of highly relevant employment at an appropriate skill level completed in the last five years.</p>
