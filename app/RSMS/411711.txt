<h1>Occupation Name: Community Worker</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 411711</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: ACWA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Facilitates community development initiatives and collective solutions within a community to address issues, needs and problems associated with recreational, health, housing, employment and other welfare matters.</p>
<p>Community workers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>assist community groups in planning, developing, maintaining and evaluating community resources, programmes and support networks</li>
<li>support, develop and evaluate strategies that encourage community participation in activities</li>
<li>research, analyse and assist council, town planning, corporate planning and environmental health departments in developing community service policies</li>
<li>communicate frequently with community groups, welfare agencies, government bodies, non-government organisations and private businesses about community services such as housing, health, welfare and recreation</li>
<li>monitor, evaluate and recommend changes to community development programmes, policies, practices or budgets</li>
<li>help raise community and public awareness regarding issues such as welfare rights by promoting, organising and helping to coordinate meetings and seminars</li>
<li>carry out administrative work which may include written correspondence, preparing submissions and reports for government bodies or other agencies and attending management meetings.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
