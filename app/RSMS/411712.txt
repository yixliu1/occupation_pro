<h1>Occupation Name: Disabilities Services Officer</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 411712</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: ACWA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Disabilities Services Officer works in a range of service units which provide education and community access to people with intellectual, physical, social and emotional disabilities.</p>
<p>Disability services officer may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>determine the needs of people with disability, including those needs that may be met through training or work experience</li>
<li>plan, develop and implement education and training programmes and check on how well they have worked</li>
<li>liaises with community groups, parents and residential workers in the development of training programs for community orientation, independent living skills, daily living activities, literacy and numeracy, communication and vocational training</li>
<li>liaises with employers and employment agencies to place clients in jobs and provide intensive on-the-job training and continuing monitoring in competitive or integrated supported employment programs</li>
<li>may establish recreation and support networks and provide recreation and community orientation training for their clients</li>
<li>may negotiate work contracts, manage the business aspects of the operation and provide occupational training and on-going support in segregated supported employment programs</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
