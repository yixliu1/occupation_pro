<h1>Occupation Name: Youth Worker</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 411716</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: ACWA</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Youth Worker assists young people as individuals or groups to solve social, emotional and financial problems in an agency framework.</p>
<p>Youth workers may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>interview young people to identify problems and act as advocates (representatives) for them, raising these issues with relevant government authorities</li>
<li>advocate for young people who have a grievance with government departments or other organisations</li>
<li>assist with developing policies relating to young people</li>
<li>provide support and advice to young people experiencing difficulties, such as family problems, unemployment, illness, drug abuse and homelessness</li>
<li>arrange and provide counselling, food, shelter or clothing</li>
<li>assess risks and provide intensive short-term crisis counselling for victims of domestic violence or child abuse</li>
<li>arrange for the referral of clients to appropriate specialists or community agencies</li>
<li>provide information about community services and resources available for young people</li>
<li>plan, conduct and evaluate programmes for young people in areas such as employment and training, education, self-development, accommodation, welfare and counselling</li>
<li>plan and organise activities such as sports, handicrafts, dancing, drama, hiking, bushwalking and holiday camps</li>
<li>establish and supervise youth clubs and small neighbourhood support groups in the local community</li>
<li>write reports and submissions requesting funding for continuing programmes and new projects</li>
<li>evaluate data relating to the effectiveness of community support services</li>
<li>work closely with teachers, social and welfare workers, local authorities, health professionals, refuge workers, parents and, in some instances, the police.</li>
<h2>Eligible applying visa subclasses</h2>
<p>This occupation belongs to Short-term Skilled Occupation List (STSOL) . STSOL consists of the list of the occupations which are high in demand in Australia. If you want to apply for Australia sponsored Permanent Residency (subclass 190) your occupation must be listed in the relevant STSOL list and you must have an invitation/sponsorship from the state government. This list is also a part of the Australia Point system. If eligible, you can live and work in Australia for an indefinite period of time.</p>
<p>If your occupation is on STSOL, you may be eligible to apply for the following visa subclasses:</p>
<li>Skilled Nominated visa (subclass 190)</li>
<li>Training visa (subclass 407)</li>
<li>Skilled Work Regional (Provisional) visa (subclass 491) - State or Territory nominated</li>
<li>Temporary Skill Shortage visa (subclass 482) - Short-term stream</li>
<li>Skilled Employer Sponsored Regional (Provisional) visa (Subclass 494)</li>
