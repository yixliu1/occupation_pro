<h1>Occupation Name: Court Orderly</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 599213</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Court Orderly provides operational support to a court or registry.</p>
<p>Court orderly (officers) may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>announce the beginning and end of court sessions</li>
<li>call on witnesses and administer the prescribed oath to jurors and witnesses</li>
<li>administer the jury roster and post court lists on notice boards</li>
<li>keep records of court processes served</li>
<li>enforce court decisions by executing court orders, and serving legal orders and documents such as summonses or subpoenas</li>
<li>maintain the orderly conduct of court and hearing rooms and adjacent areas</li>
<li>attend to judges or magistrates, escorting them from chambers to the courtroom</li>
<li>record and protect exhibits tendered in court and pass them to participants during the hearing</li>
<li>maintain security, care and control of jury panels</li>
<li>liaise with the judiciary, police department and legal professionals, and advise the public on legal procedures and practices</li>
<li>run the court registries and maintain the court files</li>
<li>open and close court and hearing room proceedings</li>
<li>organise furniture for court and hearing rooms</li>
<li>allocate rooms for use by legal practitioners</li>
<li>prepare audiovisual equipment for court and hearing rooms</li>
<li>escort prisoners to and from courtrooms</li>
<li>summon potential jurors to court.</li>

<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate IV without a highly relevant field of study and at least two years of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
