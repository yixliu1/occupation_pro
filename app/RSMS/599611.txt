<h1>Occupation Name: Insurance Investigator</h1>
<h1>ANZSCO Code: 599611</h1>
<h1>Assessment Authorities: VETASSESS</h1>
<h2>Job description </h2>
<p>Insurance Investigator conducts investigations into insurance claims to ensure their validity. Registration or licensing is required.</p>
<p>Insurance investigators may perform the following tasks:</p>
<li>estimate business losses due to fire, flood, theft, accidents and other business disruptions</li>
<li>in some cases and depending on insurance policies, source replacement goods (new or used)</li>
<li>assess compliance with policy in regard to security and customer obligations</li>
<li>inspect accident scenes and other incidents to determine causes and effects</li>
<li>interview the person making the claim to obtain the information required to assess the validity of the claim</li>
<li>interview witnesses and prepare statements</li>
<li>work out who is responsible for the accident, damage or loss</li>
<li>prepare reports on the findings of the investigations</li>
<li>work undercover to gather information</li>
<li>perform surveillance</li>
<li>put together photographic evidence.</li>

<h2>Requirements for Skills Assessment</h2>
<p>Insurance Investigator is classified as Group D occupations by Vetassess. The criteria for skills assessment of Group D occupations are the following:</p>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate IV with a highly relevant field of study and at least one year of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate IV without a highly relevant field of study and at least two years of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<li>Qualification(s) assessed at AQF Certificate III with a highly relevant field of study and at least three years of post-qualification highly relevant employment completed in the last five years;</li>
<p>According to Vetassess, if you have pre-qualification employment and if all of your employment is before your qualifications assessed at the AQF Certificate III or Certificate IV level, three years of highly relevant employment in addition to at least one year of highly relevant employment at an appropriate skill level completed in the last five years.</p>
